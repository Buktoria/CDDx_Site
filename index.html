What is Malaria?

Malaria is one of the top killer infectious diseases caused by the parasitic invasion of the bloodstream. The four types of malaria parasites, P. falciprum, P. vivax, P. ovale and P. malariae, are transmitted by female mosquitos. In 2013, 198 million cases of malaria occurred globally, causing 584000 deaths.

WHO has recommended that prompt parasitological confirmation, such as by microscopy, for patients that are suspected of having malaria. However, problems such as poor training of lab technicians, poor surveillance of disease and inadequate supplies are stalling malaria eradication.

Our mission at CDDx is to make microscopy diagnosis easier, more efficient and more accurate, to reduce the 2.7 billion USD funding costs for malaria control and eradication.

Our Solution

CDDx is a computer vision and machine learning company in the medical diagnostics market. We are developing an image pattern recognition software to be used as a smartphone application for malaria microscopy diagnosis. Manual microscopy diagnosis of malaria is significantly prone to human error, which negatively impacts costs and the health of the population. Automation of microscopy diagnosis through software designed by CDDx will eliminate the aforementioned issues.
