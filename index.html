Collaborative Disease Diagnostics

Image Recognition Software for Parasite Identification via Microscopy

Image recognition system that gives healthcare workers a portable solution for malaria diagnosis

Find Out More

Vision

Our vision is to create a collaborative data repository to stimulate future medical diagnostic innovations worldwide.

Mission

Our mission is to eradicate malaria by revolutionizing the microscopy diagnostic process.

The Problem

Malaria is one of the leading causes of death in developing countries, affecting children under five and pregnant women in particular. In 2013 alone, there were 198 million reported cases of malaria, which resulted in 584000 deaths. The burden caused by malaria has significant impact on the economic development of malarious countries; the majority of such are developing countries of Sub-Saharan Africa and India.

The current gold standard for malaria diagnosis, as outlined by World Health Organization, is microscopy. However, we have validated that manual microscopy diagnosis of malaria is highly prone to human error, which negatively impacts costs and the health of the population.

Our Solution

The Automation of microscopy diagnosis through image recognition software.

The trend for mobile phone usage in malaria-endemic regions, such as Africa, has significantly increased in recent years. Hence, we want to use smartphones and turn them into diagnostic tools that can be utilized by anybody in the world. When used in conjunction with lens attachments, the smartphone application will analyze on-site images of malaria blood smears to help provide patient diagnoses in a timely manner.

Giemsa-stained blood sample from suspected malaria patient obtained and prepared by the lab technician. Sample is viewed under microscope smartphone attachment that will take a picture of the sample. Our software will use the image captured to pinpoint problem cells, crop that portion of the image, and presented to lab technician for interpretation of problem areas.

Approach

Using technology to automate malaria diagnosis via smartphone is very efficient, economical, and accurate. The core component of our product incorporates a combination of computer vision and machine learning techniques. Once this foundation is built, minimal infrastructure will be required to maintain and use our product, allowing for a sustainable and cost-effective solution. As of date, we have successfully completed the cell counting and red blood cell identification module of the application. Our next step is to accurately identify the malaria parasite from other elements present on blood smear slides viewed through the microscope attachment. We are currently utilizing various image capture and image processing methods to extract data from the images that are characteristic of malaria.
