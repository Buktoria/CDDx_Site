Image Recognition Software for Parasite Identification via Microscopy

The Problem

Malaria is one of the leading causes of death in developing countries, affecting children under five and pregnant women in particular. In 2013 alone, there were 198 million reported cases of malaria, which resulted in 584000 deaths. The burden caused by malaria has significant impact on the economic development of malarious countries; the majority of such are developing countries of Sub-Saharan Africa and India.

The current gold standard for malaria diagnosis, as outlined by World Health Organization, is microscopy. However, we have validated that manual microscopy diagnosis of malaria is highly prone to human error, which negatively impacts costs and the health of the population.

Our Solution

The trend for mobile phone usage in malaria-endemic regions, such as Africa, has significantly increased in recent years. Hence, we want to use smartphones and turn them into diagnostic tools that can be utilized by anybody in the world. When used in conjunction with lens attachments, the smartphone application will analyze on-site images of malaria blood smears to help provide patient diagnoses in a timely manner.
